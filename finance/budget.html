Major Expenditure Heads — 10-Year Trend
FRBM Breached Every Year Since 2018-19 Tamil Nadu's fiscal deficit has exceeded the FRBM ceiling of 3% of GSDP every single year since 2018-19. The COVID year (2020-21) saw it spike to 4.45%. Even "normal" years now see 4%+. CAG additionally flags that off-budget borrowings would push the actual deficit even higher.

Committed Expenditure Crowd-Out Over 80% of TN's revenue expenditure is committed — salaries, pensions, interest payments, and subsidies. These are nearly impossible to cut in the short term. This leaves little room for development spending or fiscal adjustment.

Interest vs Capex — The Crossover
Crossover Happened in 2022-23 Interest payments exceeded capital expenditure for the first time in 2022-23 and the gap is widening. In 2024-25, TN will pay ₹0.83L Cr in interest but invest only ₹0.62L Cr in new assets.
