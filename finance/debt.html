Debt Doubled in 5 Years TN's direct debt grew from ₹5.13L Cr (2020-21) to ₹10.08L Cr (2025-26) — a near-doubling in just 5 years. Including PSU and SPV borrowings, total obligations stand at ₹13.18L Cr. TVK White Paper attributes this to: revenue deficit funding, interest-on-interest compounding, PSU losses requiring government support, and off-budget borrowings that bypass FRBM limits.

Hidden Liabilities

Off-Budget Borrowings & Contingent Liabilities

Debt that doesn't appear in the official state budget but ultimately falls on the taxpayer

Off-Budget Borrowings Borrowings routed through SPVs, PSUs, and other state entities that bypass the official FRBM fiscal deficit calculation. CAG flagged ₹1.49L Cr in off-budget borrowings in 2023-24 alone. Including these would push the effective fiscal deficit to ~6.8% of GSDP.
CAG Report on State Finances 2023-24
State Guarantees TN has extended guarantees worth ₹1.59L Cr to PSUs (2023-24). These are "contingent liabilities" — they crystallise into real debt if the PSU defaults. With TANGEDCO accumulating ₹1.79L Cr in losses, the risk is significant. FRBM allows guarantees up to 80% of GSDP — TN is well below the ceiling but the quality of these guarantees (TANGEDCO, TNSTC) is concerning.
