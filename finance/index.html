Fiscal Health Alerts

Critical indicators from white papers, CAG, and budget documents

Debt Trajectory TN's direct debt doubled in 5 years — from ₹5.13L Cr (2020-21) to ₹10.08L Cr (2025-26). Including PSU obligations: ₹13.18L Cr. Every child born in TN carries ₹1.29 lakh in state debt.
TVK White Paper, June 2026
Interest > Capital Investment Interest payments (₹0.83L Cr in 2024-25) now exceed capital expenditure (₹0.62L Cr). The state pays more to service old debt than it invests in new roads, hospitals, or schools.
TN Budget 2024-25 / CAG
FRBM Breach — Every Year Since 2018 Fiscal deficit has exceeded the FRBM limit of 3% of GSDP every year. 2023-24: 4.74%. CAG notes additional off-budget borrowings that are not reflected in the official figure.
TN Finance Dept / CAG
SOTR / GSDP at Historic Low State's Own Tax Revenue as % of GSDP fell to 5.45% — the lowest in TN's recorded history. This indicates weakening tax administration and enforcement. The state collects less from its own economy than it did 10 years ago.
TVK White Paper 2026 / Finance Dept
PSU Losses: Hidden Liabilities State PSU accumulated losses exceed ₹2.5L Cr. TANGEDCO alone: ₹1.79L Cr. These don't appear in the state budget but are ultimately the taxpayer's burden — through guaranteed loans, subsidies, and eventual bailouts.
CAG PSU Report 2023-24
Bright Spots TN is #3 on NITI Aayog SDG Index. Strong manufacturing base (auto, textiles, electronics). No Aspirational Districts. GSDP growth rebounding to ~12% post-COVID. Per capita income above national average.
